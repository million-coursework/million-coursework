[1] "On satisfying the requirements of this course, students will\r\n  have the knowledge and skills at an Advanced level of Japanese to:\r\n    1.    Demonstrate advanced language\r\n  skills in reading, writing, literary analysis and debate in Japanese and an\r\n  understanding of some of the features of Japanese literature and culture.\r\n  \r\n    2.    Interact confidently with a\r\n  variety of reading materials from representative works of pre-modern, modern\r\n  and contemporary Japanese literature. \r\n    3.    Present academic arguments\r\n  fluently and and with confidence, on issues drawn from Japanese literature\r\n  through the ages.\r\n    4.    Demonstrate information\r\n  gathering skills and the ability to compose academic essays on Japanese\r\n  literature. [2] "On satisfying the requirements of this course, students will\r\n  have the knowledge and skills at an Advanced Beginner level of spoken\r\n  Japanese to:\r\n    1. Use an active vocabulary of around 600 items, and a passive vocabulary\r\n  of around 1200 items.\r\n    2. Use grammatical points and constructions to appropriately conduct\r\n  conversations in frequently occurring situations, which include expressing\r\n  one’s thoughts, visiting one’s home, telephone conversations, celebrating\r\n  special occasions, giving advice, talking about university life, expressing\r\n  one’s emotions. \r\n    3. Use appropriate communication strategies to produce natural colloquial\r\n  Japanese. The strategies include particle omission, clause omission, echo\r\n  questions, sentence-final particles, expressions of appreciation and the use\r\n  of kedo and nante. \r\n    4. Demonstrate an understanding of cultural communication practices in\r\n  interpersonal relationships (casual speeches) and knowledge of Japan\r\n  (Japanese house, gift-giving, eating places, school system and traditional\r\n  plays)."
[3] "On satisfying the requirements of this course, students will\r\n  have the knowledge and skills at an Advanced Beginner level of modern written\r\n  Japanese to:\r\n    1. Use an active written vocabulary of around 300 kanji and a passive\r\n  vocabulary of around 500 kanji.  \r\n    2. Use grammatical points and constructions necessary to produce and\r\n  comprehend various basic sentences in a range of everyday short texts.\r\n    3. Read and compose 1200 characters long passages on topics which including\r\n  family, daily routines and normal life.\r\n    4. Demonstrate an understanding of\r\n  the cultural context gained through reading short passages on topics such as\r\n  the seasons and annual events in Japan."                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 
[4] "On satisfying the requirements of this course, students will\r\n  have the knowledge and skills at an Upper Intermediate level of spoken\r\n  Japanese to:\r\n    1. Recognise and use a broad vocabulary encompassing terms encountered in\r\n  real media taken from contemporary television and film relating to Japan.\r\n  \r\n    2. Demonstrate the appropriate linguistic skills to respond spontaneously\r\n  to contemporary issues, to state opinions, understand the opinions of others,\r\n  and use appropriate honorifics. \r\n    3. Summarise, identify and present orally on issues discussed in real media\r\n  such as commercials, podcasts and television. \r\n    4. Use communication skills and socio-cultural knowledge necessary for\r\n  giving explanations and making presentations at a sophisticated level\r\n  involving opinions and abstract ideas.\r\n    5. Demonstrate an understanding of Japan in the 21st Century including\r\n  performing arts, education, useful retailers, history, traditional arts &\r\n  crafts, nature, politics and the future of the world. "
